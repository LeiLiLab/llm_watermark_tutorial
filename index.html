Tutorials of ACL 2024

Watermarking for Large Language Model

Xuandong Zhao1, Yu-Xiang Wang2, Lei Li3,
1UC Berkeley, 2UC San Diego, 3CMU

Abstract

Generative AI has significantly advanced, particularly in natural language processing, exemplified by models like ChatGPT, but these advancements have raised concerns about misuse, such as generating fake news or plagiarizing content. This tutorial explores text watermarking as a solution, embedding detectable patterns within AI-generated text to verify its origin, unlike statistical pattern recognition methods which are prone to biases and attacks. We will cover the evolution of text watermarking, its modern techniques, and challenges, along with model watermarking for copyright protection. Participants will gain a solid understanding of watermarking methods, their practical applications, and future research directions in this critical field.

Schedule

PART I: Introduction (5 min)

Using existing slides for motivation and high-level introduction.

Presenter: Xuandong Zhao

This section provides the background for the tutorial, presenting the challenges posed by machine-written text and the potential ethical issues arising from Large Language Models (LLMs). We will introduce two primary approaches to addressing these issues: post-hoc detection and watermarking methods.

  • Potential harms caused by LLM generation
  • Background and challenges of detecting machine-written text
  • Two possible approaches:
    • Post-hoc detectors as binary classifiers
    • Watermarking methods

PART II A: Watermarking Generated Text (85 min)

Presenter: Xuandong Zhao, Yu-Xiang Wang

We delve into the process and evolution of watermarking for natural languages. We further explore watermarking methods specifically designed for Large Language Models.

  • Early stages of watermarking:
    • Synonym substitution
    • Syntactic structure restructuring
  • Neural language model-based watermarking:
    • Context-aware lexical substitution
    • Mask-infilling models for text editing
  • For traditional methods (total 5 mins)
  • Watermarking for Large Language Models:
    • Soft Watermark by Kirchenbauer et al. (2023) (20 mins)
    • Unigram Watermark by Zhao et al. (2023a) (5-10 mins) Completed
    • Gumbel Watermark by Aaronson (2023) (20 mins)
    • Theoretical results in the unified framework (15 mins) Completed
    • Undetectable Watermark by Christ et al. (2023) (5 mins)
    • Distortion-free Watermark by Kuditipudi et al. (2023) (5 mins)
    • 1-2 mins for other methods (DiPmark, Best scores, …)

— Break (30 mins) —

PART II B: Watermark Evaluation (10 mins)

We can also talk about the evaluation of LLM watermarks (10 mins)

PART III: Model Watermarking (40 min)

Presenter: Lei Li

This part explores other related watermarking methods for AI models. We will discuss various methods used to safeguard intellectual property using watermarks.

  • Copyright protection against model extraction attack:
    • CATER by He et al. (2022b) (5 mins) Completed
    • DRW by Zhao et al. (2022) (10 mins) Completed
    • Ginsew by Zhao et al. (2023b) (10 mins) Completed
    • This paper from Meta is also working on distillation.
  • Model detection against finetuning or pruning:
    • Copy, Right? by Chen et al. (2022) (5 mins) Not Started
    • IF by Xu et al. (2024) (5-10 mins) Not Started

PART IV: Post-Hoc Detection (10 min)

Presenter: Xuandong Zhao

This part focuses on post-hoc detection methods, detailing the use of binary classifiers and statistical outlier detection techniques. We will discuss the theoretical and empirical limitations.

  • Binary Classifiers:
    • Limitations of OpenAI detector (OpenAI, 2023b)
    • Traditional methods: linear/logistic regression or SVMs
    • Deep learning approaches
  • Statistical Outlier Detection:
    • Techniques based on entropy, perplexity, n-gram frequencies
    • DetectGPT by Mitchell et al. (2023)
    • Theoretical limits of detectability (Sadasivan et al., 2023)
    • Fast-DetectGPT / RADAR

PART V: Conclusion and Future Directions (10 min)

In the final section, we will provide a summary of the tutorial, discuss the best practices for implementing text watermarking, and explore potential future developments in the field of text watermarking.

  • Summary (5 mins)
  • Existing Demo / Benchmarks (5 mins)
  • Open Problems (5 mins)

PART VI: Q&A (20 mins)
